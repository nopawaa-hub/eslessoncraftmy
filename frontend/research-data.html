3. Research Methodology & Documentation Framework

Detailed overview of study design, teacher demographics, data verification processes, and longitudinal goals.

Study Sample & Demographics

The empirical research conducted for ESLessonCraft MY encompasses primary school English teachers across diverse educational settings in Malaysia to ensure broad ecological validity.

  • Target Population: Primary English Teachers (Year 1 to Year 6 KSSR).
  • School Categories: Sekolah Kebangsaan (SK), Sekolah Jenis Kebangsaan Cina (SJKC), and Sekolah Jenis Kebangsaan Tamil (SJKT).
  • Geographic Spread: Urban, suburban, and rural school districts across Malaysia.
  • Experience Levels: Novice teachers (1–3 years) through veteran panel heads (15+ years).

Data Collection Phases

Research was structured into three distinct phases to establish robust comparative baselines and measure pedagogical growth.

  • Phase 1 (Baseline Assessment): Measuring traditional manual RPH drafting duration, cognitive load, and initial HOTS integration without AI assistance.
  • Phase 2 (Intervention & Deployment): 4-week active trial utilizing ESLessonCraft MY for daily lesson planning, PBD logging, and pedagogy evaluation.
  • Phase 3 (Post-Trial Evaluation): Administration of the System Usability Scale (SUS) questionnaire and blind panel audit of generated lesson quality.

Verification & Ethical Standards

All data presented in the repositories above adheres to strict academic and educational research guidelines regarding privacy and verification.

  • Anonymization: All participating teachers and pupil records have been stripped of personally identifiable information (PII).
  • Audit Trail: Spreadsheet timestamps and revision histories are preserved to verify raw data collection dates for competition judges.
  • Instrument Reliability: Standardized Likert scales and validated usability rubrics were employed across all quantitative survey items.

Future Longitudinal Expansion (Placeholder)

As ESLessonCraft MY expands across additional school districts, our research team is preparing phase four longitudinal tracking.

  • Term-by-Term TP Progression: Correlating teacher lesson prep consistency with actual classroom mastery trends over a full academic year.
  • Automated DSKP Gap Analysis: Evaluating how AI recommendations prevent curriculum bottlenecks across difficult grammar and writing topics.
  • Panel Review Documentation: Additional research logs will be appended directly to this portal as peer-reviewed study milestones are reached.
