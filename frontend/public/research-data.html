Adoption Intent

Q10 measures stated willingness to use a reliable RPH assistant regularly — a proxy for real-world adoption intent among the sampled teachers.

Felt Administrative Burden

85% of respondents agreed or strongly agreed that they currently spend too much time formatting and writing their RPH manually (Q1, M = 4.29). Manual DSKP cross-referencing (Q2) drew similarly high fatigue at 82%.

Demand for Key Features

Auto-export to PDF/Word (Q6) reached the highest single-item demand at 90% agreement, and persistent draft storage (Q7) followed at 88% — directly informing the product's export-and-duplicate feature set.

Adoption Readiness

91% of teachers signalled they would use a reliable, user-friendly RPH assistant regularly (Q10, M = 4.45), with zero respondents disagreeing — establishing a strong pre-intervention justification for the tool.

Analysis & Interpretation

The needs assessment surveyed n = 67 Malaysian primary English teachers between 1 April and 3 June 2026 on a 5-point Likert agreement scale (1 = Strongly Disagree, 5 = Strongly Agree). Across all ten items the composite mean was M = 4.29 with an overall agreement rate of 86%, indicating uniformly strong consensus rather than isolated enthusiasm.

Respondents reported the sharpest pain around administrative time burden (Q1, agree 85%) and DSKP cross-referencing fatigue (Q2, 82%), with emotional stress from daily preparation (Q3, 82%) also prominent. These three problem-statement items anchor the design rationale for ESLessonCraft MY: they establish that the targeted inefficiencies are widely felt rather than anecdotal.

On the solution-side items, automatic document export (Q6, 90%) and persistent draft storage (Q7, 88%) emerged as the most desired capabilities, each with zero disagreement — a direct input into the product's feature prioritisation. Critically, 91% of respondents (Q10, M = 4.45) reported they would use such a tool regularly were it available, validating both the problem and the proposed intervention in a single instrument. These findings are documented here for further academic research into teacher-centred educational tooling design.

Would Recommend

Q6 measures stated willingness to recommend ESLessonCraft MY to peers — the survey's strongest proxy for perceived value and net promoter sentiment.

Preparation Time Reduction

84% of teachers agreed or strongly agreed that AI-assisted lesson planning reduced their preparation time (Q1, M = 4.22), the survey's highest single-item workload result and the largest perceived benefit.

Navigate & Use With Ease

Peer Recommendation

Analysis & Interpretation

The post-use satisfaction survey gathered n = 25 responses from teachers who had used ESLessonCraft MY, between 3 April and 4 June 2026, on the same five-point Likert agreement scale. Across the six items the composite mean was M = 4.22 and the overall agreement rate was 83%; no item fell below a 4.00 mean, indicating consistently favourable rather than polarised reception.

The most strongly endorsed outcome was peer recommendation intent (Q6, 88% agreement, M = 4.40), followed closely by perceived preparation-time reduction (Q1, 84%) and organised PBD recording (Q4, 84%). Notably, the only respondent-level disagreement in the entire survey was a single "Disagree" on workload reduction and on ease of use — suggesting the tool's value proposition landed broadly across a heterogeneous sample.

Read alongside the pre-engagement needs assessment, these results form a convergent evidence chain: the pain points teachers identified as most acute (administrative burden, time cost, DSKP cross-referencing) correspond to the post-use items that performed strongest (time reduction, ease of use, recommendation). The sample is modest (n = 25) and self-selected, so these figures are presented as formative rather than confirmatory evidence, documented for further academic research and subsequent larger-cohort replication. The raw response-level data remains available in the embedded spreadsheet above for independent re-analysis.

Analysis & Interpretation

The impact & effectiveness survey gathered n = 54 responses from teachers who had used ESLessonCraft MY, between 16 April and 30 June 2026, on a five-point Likert agreement scale across fifteen items. The composite mean was M = 4.34 and the overall agreement rate was 88%; no item fell below a 4.00 mean and every item recorded zero strong disagreement, indicating uniformly favourable reception with no systematically dissatisfied subgroup.

The strongest-performing items were overall positive teaching impact (Q15, 91%, M = 4.54), ease of use (Q1, 94%, M = 4.50), and peer recommendation intent (Q14, 93%). These three converge to suggest that teachers not only found the tool usable, but also perceived it as materially improving their teaching and were willing to advocate it to colleagues — a chain from usability through value to advocacy.

The weakest-performing item was KSSR curriculum alignment (Q5, 78%, M = 4.20), which, while still strong in absolute terms, drew the most neutral and disagree responses of any item. This is the clearest improvement signal in the dataset: teachers are broadly satisfied but want even tighter adherence to the Malaysian KSSR English curriculum standard. Workload reduction (Q10) and plan organisation (Q4) were the next-weakest, reinforcing that the platform's structural and curriculum-fit dimensions are where future development effort should concentrate. These findings are documented here for further academic research into AI-assisted lesson-planning efficacy in the Malaysian primary context.

4. Research Methodology & Documentation Framework

Detailed overview of study design, instruments, teacher demographics, data verification processes, and longitudinal research goals.

Instruments & Sample

Two complementary Google Forms instruments frame the empirical record. Both used a five-point Likert agreement scale (1 = Strongly Disagree → 5 = Strongly Agree); every headline figure on this portal is computed directly from the corresponding response set rather than reported from memory.

  • Needs Assessment Survey (pre-engagement): 10 items, n = 67, collected 1 Apr–3 Jun 2026. Measured administrative burden, DSKP cross-referencing fatigue, and demand for specific tool capabilities.
  • User Satisfaction & Usability Survey (post-use): 6 items, n = 25, collected 3 Apr–4 Jun 2026. Measured perceived time savings, ease of use, workload reduction, and peer recommendation intent.
  • Scale & Scoring: Agreement rate = (% Agree + % Strongly Agree); composite mean = arithmetic mean of item means. Snapshots captured 2026-07-15.

Study Sample & Demographics

The research encompasses primary school English teachers across diverse educational settings in Malaysia to support broad ecological validity.

  • Target Population: Primary English Teachers (Year 1 to Year 6 KSSR).
  • School Categories: Sekolah Kebangsaan (SK), Sekolah Jenis Kebangsaan Cina (SJKC), and Sekolah Jenis Kebangsaan Tamil (SJKT).
  • Geographic Spread: Urban, suburban, and rural school districts across Malaysia.
  • Experience Levels: Novice teachers (1–3 years) through veteran panel heads (15+ years).

Data Collection Phases

Research was structured into three phases to establish comparative baselines and measure pedagogical growth.

  • Phase 1 (Needs Assessment): Baseline survey of manual RPH drafting pain points, cognitive load, and feature demand without AI assistance.
  • Phase 2 (Intervention & Deployment): Active trial utilising ESLessonCraft MY for daily lesson planning, PBD logging, and pedagogy evaluation.
  • Phase 3 (Post-Trial Evaluation): Administration of the post-use satisfaction & usability survey; supplementary System Usability Scale (SUS) scoring where collected.

Verification & Ethical Standards

All data presented in the repositories above adheres to academic and educational research guidelines regarding privacy and verification.

  • Anonymization: All participating teachers and pupil records have been stripped of personally identifiable information (PII).
  • Audit Trail: Spreadsheet timestamps and revision histories are preserved to verify raw data collection dates for reproducibility and peer review.
  • Instrument Reliability: Standardised Likert scales and validated usability rubrics were employed across all quantitative survey items.
  • Limitations: The post-use sample (n = 25) is self-selected and modest; findings are treated as formative evidence documented for further academic research and larger-cohort replication.

Future Longitudinal Research

As ESLessonCraft MY expands across additional school districts, our research team is preparing phase four longitudinal tracking.

  • Term-by-Term TP Progression: Correlating teacher lesson prep consistency with actual classroom mastery trends over a full academic year.
  • Automated DSKP Gap Analysis: Evaluating how AI recommendations prevent curriculum bottlenecks across difficult grammar and writing topics.
  • Open Research Logs: Additional anonymised datasets and replication materials will be appended to this portal as peer-reviewed study milestones are reached, supporting further academic research.

Data Availability Statement

The two survey response datasets documented on this portal are available for independent re-analysis.

  • Raw responses: Live, read-only Google Sheets linked in the Needs Survey and User Satisfaction sections above retain full per-respondent rows including timestamps.
  • Computed figures: Every percentage, mean, and sample size shown on this page is computed client-side from an embedded response snapshot, so the displayed values are fully traceable to the underlying data.
  • Reuse: Researchers wishing to reuse these datasets for further academic research are invited to cite this repository; the underlying forms remain open for replication.
